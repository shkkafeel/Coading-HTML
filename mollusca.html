Phylum Mollusca

Phylum: Mollusca

Kingdom: Animalia

Information

Molluscs [also know as mollusks] are soft-bodied, bilaterally symmetrical, segmented, coelomate animals; usually shelled having a mantle, ventral foot, anterior head, and a dorsal visceral mass.

Characteristics

Characteristics are mentioned below:

1. They are essentially aquatic mostly marine, few freshwater and some terrestrial form.

2. They may be found as hidden parasites in the interior of other animals.

3. They vary in size from giant squids and clams to little snails, a millimeter long.

4. They have at least two characters radula and mantle not found elsewhere.

5. The mouth contains a file-like rasping organ for feeding, called radula.

6. The body consists of head, foot, mantle, and the visceral mass.

7. The body is commonly protected by an exoskeleton calcareous shell of one or more piece secreted by the mantle.

8. Respiration is direct or by gills or lungs or both.

9. Haemocyanin is their respiratory pigments.

10. Excretion is by paired metanephridia (kidney).

11. Sense organs consist of eyes, statocysts, and receptors for touch, smell, and taste.

12. Sexes are usually separate (dioecious) but some are monoecious (hermaphroditic).

13. Fertilization is external or internal.

14. They have tissue-system grade of body organization.

15. Development is direct or with metamorphosis through the trochophore stage called veliger larva.
